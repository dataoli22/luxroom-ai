# 🏡 The Complete Student Housing Guide for Luxembourg

A practical, end-to-end guide to finding a room in Luxembourg as an international student — covering the places **LuxRoom AI can't reach for you**: private Facebook & WhatsApp groups, university housing, word-of-mouth, plus the real-world stuff nobody explains (deposits, domiciliation, scams).

> **How this fits with the app.** LuxRoom AI automatically watches the public listing websites (athome, Immotop, Wortimmo, HousingAnywhere, and more) so you don't have to. But a huge share of student rooms are found in **closed Facebook/WhatsApp groups, through the university, and by word of mouth** — places an app simply can't log into for you. Use the app for the websites; use this guide for everything else. Together they cover the whole market.

---

## ⏰ Start early — the single most important tip

Demand hugely outstrips supply, especially around the university. **Start looking 8–12 weeks before you arrive.**

- **September (autumn) start →** begin in **June/July**.
- **February (spring) start →** begin in **November**.

The University of Luxembourg says it can only house roughly **15–20% of students who apply**, so assume you'll need the private market too. Rooms get taken within hours of being posted — reply fast, and have your documents ready (see the [checklist](#-viewing--application-checklist)).

## 1. 🎓 University of Luxembourg housing (apply first)

If you're a uni student, apply for a university room **as soon as you're admitted** — it's the cheapest and safest option, even though places are limited.

- **Accommodation page:** [uni.lu → Accommodation](https://www.uni.lu/life-en/accommodation/) and [residences](https://www.uni.lu/life-en/accommodation/residences/)
- The **SEVE Housing Office** (Service des Études et de la Vie Étudiante) allocates rooms, mostly near the **Belval** and **Kirchberg** campuses.
- Apply early and accept quickly if offered — waitlists are long.

Doing an internship or a non-uni programme? You usually can't use uni housing, so go straight to the sections below.

## 3. 💬 WhatsApp groups (invite-only — here's how to get in)

WhatsApp housing groups are real and active, but they're **invite-only**, so no app or website can list them. Get the invite links from people, not search engines:

- **Your university's international / welcome office** — ask them directly for the student housing WhatsApp/Discord.
- **ESN Luxembourg (Erasmus Student Network)** — they run arrival groups every semester. Find them on [Instagram/Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/search/top?q=esn%20luxembourg) and ask to be added.
- **Your course/programme cohort chat** — new students share room leads constantly; ask seniors in your programme.
- **Country/community groups** — many nationalities run a Luxembourg WhatsApp community (e.g. "Indians in Luxembourg", "Portuguese in Luxembourg"). Ask your embassy's student network or search Facebook for the community, then request the WhatsApp link.
- **Residence & building groups** — once you're in a shared house, there's often a tenants' WhatsApp that hears about rooms opening up first.

> Post a short intro when you join: who you are, your budget, move-in date, and how long you're staying. Politeness + specifics = replies.

## 5. 📍 Where to live — areas & realistic budgets

Most students search only in Luxembourg City and overpay. Look wider — the trains are excellent.

| Area | Room price /month | Why consider it |
|---|---|---|
| **Luxembourg City** (Bonnevoie, Gare, Limpertsberg…) | €650–950 | Most convenient, most expensive. Tram + buses everywhere. |
| ⭐ **North — CFL Line 10** (Mersch, Lintgen, Walferdange) | **€380–580** | The hidden gem: direct trains to Gare/Kirchberg in ~25–35 min, roughly *half* the city price. Most students don't know about it. |
| **Suburbs / communes** (Bertrange, Strassen) | €500–750 | Quiet, good buses, often furnished rooms in shared houses. |
| **South — Esch / Belval** | €380–600 | Right by the University's main campus. Ideal if you study at Belval. |

> **Public transport is free** across the whole country — so a cheaper room 30 minutes out costs you nothing extra to commute from. LuxRoom AI factors commute time into every score.

## 6. 🚩 Avoiding scams (read this before you pay anything)

Scams are common in the Facebook groups. The pattern is always the same: a *too-good* room, a landlord who's "abroad" and can't show it, and a request to **wire a deposit to "reserve" it**. Protect yourself:

- **Never pay anything — deposit, "reservation", first month — before you (or someone you trust) have physically visited the room** and met the landlord.
- **Never send money by Western Union / bank transfer to a stranger** to hold a room. Real landlords don't need that.
- **Reverse-image-search the photos** (Google Images). Scam ads reuse stock/other listings' photos.
- Be suspicious of prices well below the ranges above, pressure to "decide now", or landlords who refuse a video call / live visit.
- Insist on a **written lease (contrat de bail)** before paying the deposit.
- If you're abroad, prefer platforms with **payment protection** (HousingAnywhere, Spotahome, Uniplaces) or university housing.

> LuxRoom AI never sends money and never asks for it — it only drafts *introduction messages* that you approve. Any request for money always comes from the landlord, so apply this checklist every time.

## 7. 📝 The paperwork (deposit, contract, domiciliation)

Luxembourg has a few things international students often don't expect:

- **Deposit (garantie locative):** usually **1–3 months' rent**, held until you leave. Budget for it on top of the first month.
- **Agency fee:** if you rent through an agency, expect a one-off fee of about **one month's rent + VAT**. Renting a room directly from a landlord usually avoids this.
- **The lease (bail):** get it in writing, in a language you understand. Note the notice period and what's included (charges/utilities, internet, furniture).
- **⚠️ Domiciliation — crucial for your visa/residence permit.** To register as a resident (and for a residence permit), you need a **real registered address**. Some landlords/rooms **don't allow domiciliation** — for a student who needs to register, that's a dealbreaker. **Always ask "Is domiciliation possible?" before committing.** LuxRoom AI flags this on each listing when it can detect it.
- **Register at the commune:** after moving in, you must make a *déclaration d'arrivée* at your local commune (town hall), usually within **8 days**. The landlord provides a proof-of-residence document for this.

## 8. ✅ Viewing & application checklist

Have these ready so you can say "yes" the moment you find the right room (good rooms go in hours):

- [ ] A short intro about yourself (name, what you study/where you work, how long you'll stay, non-smoker etc.)
- [ ] Passport / ID
- [ ] Proof of enrolment (uni admission letter) or work contract
- [ ] Proof you can pay (scholarship letter, bank statement, or a guarantor)
- [ ] Money ready for the **deposit + first month** (but only paid *after* a real visit + signed lease)
- [ ] Questions to ask at the viewing: *Is domiciliation allowed? What's included in the rent? Deposit amount? Notice period? Who else lives here?*

## 9. 🗺️ Your week-by-week plan

1. **12 weeks out:** Apply for university housing. Join every Facebook group and get into the WhatsApp/ESN groups. Set up **LuxRoom AI** so the websites are covered automatically.
2. **8 weeks out:** Post your own "looking for a room" ads in the groups. Reply to everything that fits — fast. Start booking viewings (video calls if you're abroad).
3. **4 weeks out:** Prioritise rooms that allow **domiciliation**. Confirm one, get the lease in writing, pay the deposit only after a visit.
4. **On arrival:** Register at your **commune** within 8 days. Join your building's tenant chat — you'll hear about the next opening first.
